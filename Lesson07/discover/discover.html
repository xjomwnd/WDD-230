Preston Idaho

Introductory History

Preston, Idaho, a small agricultural community in northern Cache Valley, in early times was a hunting ground for Indians who camped nearby. The first white men to visit the area were trappers, immigrants and explorers. Mormon settlers had pushed to Its borders by 1860.

City Demographics

According to the United States Census Bureau, Preston city has a total area of 6.66 square miles (17.25 km2), of which, 6.65 square miles (17.22 km2) is land and 0.01 square miles (0.03 km2) is water.

Ethinicity in Preston

African American, 0.7% Native American, 0.2% Asian, 0.1% Pacific Islander, 3.3% from other races, and 1.7% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 7.5% of the population.

Preston Area Locality

Preston, Idaho is one of the best kept secrets of the West. Nestled on the Idaho side of beautiful Cache Valley and surrounded by towering mountains on each side.
