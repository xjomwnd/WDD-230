Preston History

Preston, Idaho, a small agricultural community in northern Cache Valley, in early times was a hunting ground for Indians who camped nearby. The first white men to visit the area were trappers, immigrants and explorers. Mormon settlers had pushed to Its borders by 1860.

Preston Idaho Demographics

According to the United States Census Bureau, Preston city has a total area of 6.66 square miles (17.25 km2), of which, 6.65 square miles (17.22 km2) is land and 0.01 square miles (0.03 km2) is water. Nearby is the Cub River Canyon, which is a popular recreation area.

Ethnic Groups in Preston City

African American, 0.7% Native American, 0.2% Asian, 0.1% Pacific Islander, 3.3% from other races, and 1.7% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 7.5% of the population.

Preston City Locality

Preston, Idaho is one of the best kept secrets of the West. Nestled on the Idaho side of beautiful Cache Valley and surrounded by towering mountains on each side, it is unbeatable for recreation opportunities, has that small town feeling, and is close to whatever you need.
